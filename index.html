The Assessment

VO2Max

VO₂ Max

This test measures how efficiently your body uses oxygen during exercise.

A higher VO₂ max is linked to a longer life and lower risk of disease.

Epigenic Clocks

Epigenetic Clocks

Analyze DNA methylation to estimate biological age.

These molecular markers reflect how behaviors, lifestyle and environment impact gene regulation, offering insight into age-related risk.

AI Retinal Analysis

Retinal-Based Age Test

The noninvasive test uses AI to detect age-related changes in the blood vessels in your eyes

This gives us insights into cardiovascular and metabolic aging.

PAI-1

Plasminogen Activator Inhibitor-1 (PAI-1)

PAI-1 is a protein that regulates blood clotting and reflects cellular aging.

Elevated PAI-1 levels are associated with accelerated aging.

Endothelial Function

Endothelial Function

Assesses nitric oxide production and vessel flexibility.

Reduced endothelial function is linked to vascular stiffness, hypertension, and early cardiovascular risk.

Body Composition

DXA Scan

The scan measures fat and lean masses.

Excess fat is associated with chronic inflammation and aging.

Pulse Wave Velocity

Pulse Wave Velocity

The test checks fast blood pressure waves move through your arteries.

This tells us how stiff your arteries are, which is a key indicator of vascular aging.

A higher velocity likely means you have stiffer arteries and a higher risk of heart and vascular disease.

A biological age assessment is a wellness consultation that is not covered by insurance. A biological age assessment is not a substitute for medical advice about the diagnosis, cure treatment, mitigation or prevention of any disease or other medical condition. Unless advised by your healthcare professional, biological age should not be used to determine or alter any health or medical treatments. Your biological age refers to the age as estimated by your physical health, which can be influenced by genetics, lifestyle and diet.
